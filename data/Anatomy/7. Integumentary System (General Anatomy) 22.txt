Question: The superficial fascia is dense in all places except:
Options:
a) Scalp
b) Sole of foot
c) Toe of foot
d) Areola of breast
Correct Option:
Ans. Scalp
Explanation: The superficial fascia is a connective tissue layer under the skin, dense in areas like soles and toes for protection, but in the scalp, it is loose to allow free mobility of the skin over the skull.

b) Nose

Question: Which of the following is one of the appendages of skin:
Options:
a) Stratum corneum
b) Epidermis
c) Dermis
d) Hair follicles
Correct Option:
Ans. Hair follicles
Explanation: Hair follicles are epidermal appendages that produce hair. Other appendages include sebaceous and sweat glands, while stratum corneum, epidermis, and dermis are structural layers, not appendages.
