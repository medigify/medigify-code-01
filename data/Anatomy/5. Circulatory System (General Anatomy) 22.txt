Question: AV shunt is present in:
Options:
a) Lips
b) Deltoid
c) Muscle
d) Skin
Correct Option:
Ans. Lips
Explanation: Arteriovenous (AV) shunts are present in areas like the lips, fingertips, and nose, allowing blood to bypass capillaries for thermoregulation. They are absent in muscles or skin in general.

Question: Regarding blood vessels all of the following statements are false except:
Options:
a) In the continuous capillaries, endothelium has small pores
b) Sinusoids are dilated veins
c) Aorta is an elastic artery
d) Capillaries have semipermeable valves
Correct Option:
Ans. Aorta is an elastic artery
Explanation: The aorta is an elastic artery, containing elastic fibers in its tunica media that help maintain continuous blood flow during diastole. Continuous capillaries have no large pores, sinusoids are not veins, and capillaries lack valves.

Question: Following are the end arteries except:
Options:
a) Central artery of retina
b) Coronary artery
c) Spleen
d) Kidney
Correct Option:
Ans. Coronary artery
Explanation: End arteries supply a single region without significant collateral circulation, like the central artery of retina, spleen, and kidney. The coronary arteries have multiple anastomoses, so they are not purely end arteries.

Question: Sinusoidal capillaries are found in:
Options:
a) Kidney
b) Liver
c) Renal artery
d) Splenic artery
Correct Option:
Ans. Liver
Explanation: Sinusoids are large, irregular vascular spaces with a discontinuous endothelium, found in the liver, spleen, and bone marrow, allowing free exchange of large molecules and cells.

Question: Regarding blood vessels:
Options:
a) large arteries are elastic having valves
b) Capillaries have thick muscular coat
c) Sinusoids are large vascular irregular spaces
d) Veins of lower limb have no valves
Correct Option:
Ans. Sinusoids are large vascular irregular spaces
Explanation: Sinusoids are dilated, irregular channels facilitating exchange between blood and tissues. Large arteries have elastic walls, capillaries are thin-walled, and lower limb veins have valves.

Question: Portal system is formed by two sets of:
Options:
a) Arteries
b) Veins
c) Arterioles
d) Capillaries
Correct Option:
Ans. Arterioles
Explanation: A portal system consists of two capillary beds in series, connected by a vein or arteriole. Examples include the hepatic portal system and hypophyseal portal system.
