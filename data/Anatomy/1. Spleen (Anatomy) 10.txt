Question: Spleen rests on:
Options:
a) Splenorenal ligament
b) Gastrosplenic ligament
c) Phrenicocolic ligament
d) 9-12 ribs
Correct Option:
Ans. Phrenicocolic ligament
Explanation: The phrenicocolic ligament (also known as the sustentaculum lienis) is a fold of peritoneum extending from the splenic flexure of the colon to the diaphragm. It acts as a structural "shelf" or floor that supports the weight of the spleen in the upright position.

Question: What is the anatomical location of spleen:
Options:
a) Left hypochondrium
b) Right hypogastrium
c) Left flank
d) Right flank
Correct Option:
Ans. Left hypochondrium
Explanation: The spleen is located in the left hypochondrium, tucked deep under the protection of the diaphragm. It is entirely covered by the rib cage (specifically ribs 9 through 11), making it non-palpable in a healthy individual unless it is significantly enlarged.

Question: The left rib that lies parallel to the long axis of spleen:
Options:
a) 8th
b) 9th
c) 10th
d) 11th
Correct Option:
Ans. 10th
Explanation: The long axis of the spleen lies parallel to the 10th rib. This orientation is clinically significant because as the spleen enlarges (splenomegaly), it typically grows along this axis toward the right iliac fossa, crossing the midline of the abdomen.

Question: Spleen is supplied by splenic artery which is a branch of:
Options:
a) Superior mesenteric artery
b) Right gastroepiploic artery
c) Celiac trunk
d) Left gastroepiploic artery
Correct Option:
Ans. Celiac trunk
Explanation: The splenic artery is the largest and most tortuous branch of the celiac trunk. It travels along the superior border of the pancreas, eventually entering the lienorenal ligament to reach the splenic hilum, where it divides into several segmental branches.

Question: Which of the following ligaments connects the spleen to the left kidney:
Options:
a) Gastrosplenic ligament
b) Lienorenal ligament
c) Splenocolic ligament
d) Splenophrenic ligament
Correct Option:
Ans. Lienorenal ligament
Explanation: The lienorenal (or splenorenal) ligament is a double fold of peritoneum that connects the hilum of the spleen to the left kidney. It is a vital surgical landmark because it serves as the "highway" for the splenic vessels and the tail of the pancreas.

Question: Which of the following structures is located anterior to the spleen:
Options:
a) Left kidney
b) Pancreas
c) Stomach
d) Transverse colon
Correct Option:
Ans. Stomach
Explanation: The stomach is the primary structure located anterior to the spleen. The visceral surface of the spleen actually bears a "gastric impression" where it rests against the fundus and the greater curvature of the stomach.

Question: Which of the following is a content of splenorenal ligament:
Options:
a) Renal artery
b) Splenic artery
c) Gastroepiploic artery
d) Phrenic nerve
Correct Option:
Ans. Splenic artery
Explanation: The splenic artery and vein are the most critical contents of the splenorenal ligament. Surgeons must be extremely careful when clamping this ligament during a splenectomy to avoid accidentally damaging the tail of the pancreas, which also sits within these folds.

Question: Posteriorly, spleen is related to which ribs:
Options:
a) 9th - 11th ribs
b) only 9th rib
c) 10th - 12th ribs
d) only 11th rib
Correct Option:
Ans. 9th - 11th ribs
Explanation: Posteriorly, the spleen is related to the 9th, 10th, and 11th ribs on the left side. It is separated from them only by the diaphragm and the costodiaphragmatic recess of the pleura, which explains why fractured ribs in this area often result in splenic laceration.

Question: Oesophageal varices are caused by which one of the following:
Options:
a) Portal hypertension
b) Pulmonary hypertension
c) Systemic hypertension
d) Enlarged liver pressing on portal vein
Correct Option:
Ans. Portal hypertension
Explanation: Portal hypertension causes blood to back up into portosystemic anastomoses. In the lower esophagus, the high-pressure portal blood diverts into the submucosal veins (left gastric vein to esophageal veins), causing them to dilate into oesophageal varices that are prone to life-threatening rupture.

Question: A 25 year old young man presented in emergency with blunt trauma abdomen. He is in shock with blood pressure 70/40; pulse 124/min. On examination, abdomen is tense & tender more on left upper abdomen. Which solid organ is most likely to be injured:
Options:
a) Liver
b) Spleen
c) Diaphragm
d) Kidney
Correct Option:
Ans. Spleen
Explanation: In cases of blunt trauma to the left upper abdomen, the spleen is the most likely solid organ to rupture. Because its "red pulp" is essentially a blood-filled sponge enclosed in a thin capsule, injury leads to massive internal bleeding, rapid onset of shock, and abdominal tenderness.
