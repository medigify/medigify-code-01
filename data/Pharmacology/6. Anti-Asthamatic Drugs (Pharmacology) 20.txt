Question: A 12-year-old girl, known patient of asthma, presents to the emergency room with complaints of cough, dyspnea & wheezing after visiting a riding stable. Which is the most appropriate drug to rapidly reverse her bronchoconstriction:
Options:
a) Inhaled fluticasone
c) IV propranolol
b) Inhaled albuterol
d) Inhaled beclomethasone
Correct Option:
Ans. Inhaled albuterol
Explanation: A 12-year-old girl with asthma experiencing acute bronchoconstriction after allergen exposure should be treated with inhaled albuterol, a short-acting β2 agonist, because it rapidly relaxes bronchial smooth muscle. Inhaled corticosteroids like fluticasone or beclomethasone act slowly and are preventive, not for immediate relief, while IV propranolol is a β-blocker and would worsen bronchospasm.

Question: Which of the following pharmacologic agents antagonizes adenosine:
Options:
a) Terbutaline
b) Glucocorticoids
c) Cromolyn sodium
d) Theophylline
Correct Option:
Ans. Theophylline
Explanation: Theophyline works by blocking adenosine receptors in bronchial smooth muscle. Adenosine normally promotes bronchoconstriction and release of inflammatory mediators, so when theophylline blocks these receptors, it leads to bronchodilation and improved airflow. Terbutaline is a β2 agonist that causes bronchodilation by increasing cAMP, not by blocking adenosine. Glucocorticoids reduce inflammation by altering gene expression; they do not act on adenosine receptors. Cromolyn sodium stabilizes mast cells and prevents mediator release but does not block adenosine.
