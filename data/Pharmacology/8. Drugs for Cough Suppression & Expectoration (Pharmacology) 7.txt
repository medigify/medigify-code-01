Drugs for Cough Suppression & Expectoration
Question: Bromhexine acts by:
Options:
a) Inhibiting cough center
b) Irritating gastric mucosa and reflexly increasing bronchial secretion
c) Depolymerizing mucopolysaccharides present in sputum
d) Both B and C
Correct Option:
Ans. Both B and C
Explanation: Bromhexine acts by both irritating gastric mucosa and depolymerizing mucopolysaccharides (correct: D) because it increases bronchial secretions reflexly and also breaks down thick mucus, making it easier to expectorate. It does not inhibit the cough center, so option A is wrong, and since it does both B and C, choosing either alone would be incomplete.

Question: A 32-year-old man with a history of opioid addiction presents with cough due to viral respiratory system infection. Which symptomatic treatment is appropriate for cough in this patient:
Options:
a) Montelukast
c) Codeine (opioid)
b) Benzonatate
d) Cromolyn
Correct Option:
Ans. Benzonatate
Explanation: Benzonatate is correct for a patient with opioid addiction because it suppresses cough peripherally without risk of dependence. Codeine is an opioid and should be avoided in such patients, while montelukast and cromolyn are anti-asthmatic drugs and not useful for symptomatic relief of acute cough.

Question: Expectorants are:
Options:
a) Bronchial secretion enhancers
c) Anti-histamines
b) Antitussive
d) Bronchodilators
Correct Option:
Ans. Bronchial secretion enhancers
Explanation: Expectorants are bronchial secretion enhancers (correct) because they increase the volume and reduce the viscosity of secretions to help clear mucus. They are not antitussives (which suppress cough), not antihistamines, and not bronchodilators.

Question: Which drug acts as centrally and peripherally acting antitussives:
Options:
a) Codeine
c) Prenoxdiazine
b) Both A & D
d) Benzonatate
Correct Option:
Ans. Both A & D
Explanation: Both codeine and benzonatate are correct because codeine acts centrally on the cough center, while benzonatate acts peripherally by anesthetizing stretch receptors in the respiratory tract. Prenoxdiazine is mainly peripheral, so it does not fulfill both actions.

Question: Which of the following is the drug of choice in the treatment of productive cough:
Options:
a) Dextromethorphan
c) Pholcodine
b) Bromhexine
d) Noscapine
Correct Option:
Ans. Bromhexine
Explanation: Bromhexine is correct in productive cough because it helps liquefy mucus and promotes its removal. Dextromethorphan, pholcodine, and noscapine are antitussives, which suppress cough and are therefore inappropriate when mucus needs to be expelled.

Question: There are different types of antitussive drugs. Which of the following is a mucolytic:
Options:
a) Benzonatate
c) Acetylcysteine
b) Codeine
d) Sodium iodide
Correct Option:
Ans. Acetylcysteine
Explanation: Acetylcysteine is correct as a mucolytic because it breaks disulfide bonds in mucus, reducing its viscosity. Benzonatate and codeine are antitussives, and sodium iodide is an expectorant, not a true mucolytic.

Question: Which drug depolymerizes mucopolysaccharides of mucus:
Options:
a) Guaiphenesin
c) Bromhexine
b) Nascopine
d) Diphenhydramine
Correct Option:
Ans. Bromhexine
Explanation: Bromhexine is correct because it depolymerizes mucopolysaccharides in mucus, making it less viscous. Guaiphenesin is an expectorant that increases secretions but does not directly break mucus structure, noscapine is an antitussive, and diphenhydramine is an antihistamine.
