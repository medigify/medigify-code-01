Question: Which route of administration is commonly used for insulin:
Options:
a) Intravenous
b) Intramuscular
c) Subcutaneous
d) Oral
Correct Option:
Ans. Subcutaneous
Explanation: Insulin is a protein that would be degraded by digestive enzymes if taken orally, and it requires a route that allows for consistent absorption. The subcutaneous route is preferred because it provides a slow and steady release into the bloodstream, which is ideal for managing blood glucose levels throughout the day. Intravenous administration is typically reserved for emergency settings like diabetic ketoacidosis, while intramuscular injections are generally avoided due to faster and less predictable absorption rates.

Options:
a) Passive diffusion
b) Facilitated diffusion
c) Active transport
d) Pinocytosis
Correct Option:
Ans. Pinocytosis
Explanation: Most drugs are absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract via passive diffusion, facilitated diffusion, or active transport systems. Pinocytosis, the process of a cell "drinking" or engulfing fluid, is an extremely rare and inefficient mechanism for drug absorption, generally reserved for very large molecules or specialized proteins. Because the vast majority of therapeutic agents rely on smaller, more efficient transport pathways, pinocytosis is not considered a standard mechanism for GI drug uptake.

Question: A drug that binds to the inactive state of a receptor & decreases constitutive activity is known as:
Options:
a) Partial agonist
b) Inverse agonist
c) Antagonist
d) Allosteric agonist
Correct Option:
Ans. Inverse agonist
Explanation: An inverse agonist is a drug that binds to the same receptor as an agonist but produces a pharmacological response opposite to that of the agonist. It specifically targets receptors that exhibit constitutive activity, meaning they are active even in the absence of a ligand, and forces them into an inactive state. This differs from a standard antagonist, which merely blocks the effects of an agonist without affecting the receptor's baseline activity.
