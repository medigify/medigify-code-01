Question: Local anesthetics cause vasodilation. Their rate and duration of action can be increased & toxicity can be decreased by:
Options:
a) Administering another vasodilator anesthetic
b) Increasing dose of local anesthetic
c) Administering vasoconstrictor like epinephrine
d) Decreasing dose of that vasodilating anesthetic
Correct Option:
Ans. Administering vasoconstrictor like epinephrine
Explanation: Local anesthetics generally cause vasodilation, which increases their rate of absorption into the bloodstream, thereby shortening their duration and increasing systemic toxicity. Administering a vasoconstrictor like epinephrine is the correct strategy because it restricts local blood flow, keeping the anesthetic at the injection site longer while reducing the risk of systemic side effects.

Question: Characteristic properties of local anesthetics include all of the following EXCEPT:
Options:
a) An increase in membrane refractory period
c) Effects on vascular tone
b) Blockade of voltage-dependent sodium channels
d) Preferential binding to resting channels
Correct Option:
Ans. Preferential binding to resting channels
Explanation: Local anesthetics function by blocking voltage-dependent sodium (Na+) channels on the inner surface of nerve membranes, which prevents the influx of ions necessary for action potential conduction. They exhibit state-dependent blockade, meaning they bind preferentially to channels in their open (activated) or inactivated states, rather than resting channels. Blocking the sodium/potassium pump or calcium channels would not produce the rapid, reversible loss of sensation characteristic of these drugs.
