Options:
a) Diphenhydramine
c) Promethazine

Question: Which of the following is a recognized adverse effect of cimetidine:
Options:
a) Blurred vision
b) Diarrhea
c) Sedation
d) P450 hepatic enzyme inhibition
Correct Option:
Ans. P450 hepatic enzyme inhibition
Explanation: Cimetidine is well known to inhibit cytochrome P450 enzymes in the liver, which can increase the levels of many other drugs (e.g., warfarin, phenytoin). This is a classic and high-yield adverse effect. Blurred vision is not a characteristic adverse effect of cimetidine; it is more commonly associated with anticholinergic drugs. Diarrhea can occur with many drugs but is not a defining or high-yield adverse effect of cimetidine. Sedation is typical of first-generation H₁ antihistamines, not H₂ blockers like cimetidine.

Question: Antiemetic action of H1 antagonist is due to the blockade of:
Options:
a) M1 receptors
c) Alpha 1 receptors
b) Beta 1 receptors
d) Serotonin receptors
Correct Option:
Ans. M1 receptors
Explanation: The antiemetic effect of H1 blockers, such as diphenhydramine or meclizine, is due to their ability to block muscarinic M1 receptors in the vestibular system and the chemoreceptor trigger zone of the brain. This is why these drugs are effective in controlling nausea, vomiting, and motion sickness. Other receptor systems, like beta-1, alpha-1, or serotonin receptors, are not primarily involved in this antiemetic action.

Question: Which of the following will result from blockade of H2 receptors:
Options:
a) Increased cAMP in cardiac muscle
c) Decreased cAMP in gastric mucosa
b) Decreased channel opening in enteric nerves
d) Increased IP3 in platelets
Correct Option:
Ans. Decreased cAMP in gastric mucosa
Explanation: When considering H2 receptor antagonists, their main effect is to reduce gastric acid secretion. H2 receptors on parietal cells stimulate acid production through cAMP; therefore, blocking these receptors leads to decreased cAMP levels in gastric mucosa. Effects on cardiac muscle cAMP, enteric nerve channels, or platelet IP3 are not related to H2 blockade.

Question: Which drug is used in the treatment of Urticaria:
Options:
a) Antihistamine
c) Steroid
b) Epinephrine
d) Beta blockers
Correct Option:
Ans. Antihistamine
Explanation: In conditions like urticaria and hay fever, H1 antihistamines are first-line treatment because they relieve itching, hives, and nasal symptoms by blocking histamine at peripheral H1 receptors. Steroids are only used in more severe or refractory cases, while epinephrine is reserved for anaphylaxis. Beta-blockers have no role in these allergic conditions.

Question: A 30-year-old woman who is a beekeeper always gets sting bites by bees and develops rash. She also has some food allergies. Which drug should she always keep with her:
Options:
a) Antihistamine
b) Epinephrine
c) Steroid
d) Beta blockers
Correct Option:
Ans. Epinephrine
Explanation: Patients with a history of severe allergic reactions, such as a beekeeper who develops rashes or anaphylaxis from bee stings, should always carry epinephrine. Antihistamines or steroids cannot act quickly enough to prevent life-threatening reactions, and beta-blockers are contraindicated in anaphylaxis because they can worsen hypotension and block epinephrine’s effects.
