Question: Which one of the following is not a cause of coagulation defects:
Options:
a) Platelet deficiency
b) Liver disease
c) Vitamin K deficiency
d) Hemophilia
Correct Option:
Ans. Platelet deficiency
Explanation: Platelet deficiency is a platelet disorder, not a coagulation factor defect. Coagulation defects involve abnormalities in clotting factors, such as in liver disease, vitamin K deficiency, or hemophilia. Platelets are part of primary hemostasis.

a) Abnormal clotting factors
b) Genetic defect

d) Absence of clotting factors
Correct Option:
