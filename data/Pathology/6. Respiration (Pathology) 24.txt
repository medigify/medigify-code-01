Question: Type of emphysema commonly associated with cigarette smoking:
Options:
a) Centriacinar
b) Panacinar
c) Distal acinar
d) Irregular
Correct Option:
Ans. Centriacinar
Explanation: Centriacinar emphysema affects the respiratory bronchioles and is strongly linked to smoking. It predominantly involves the upper lobes. Smoking increases neutrophils and macrophages → elastase release → destruction of alveolar walls.

Question: When IgE on a mast cell Fcε receptor is cross linked by an allergen, the mast cell responds by:
Options:
a) Undergoing apoptosis
b) Presenting antigen to Th cells
c) Secreting more IgE
d) Secreting histamine
Correct Option:
Ans. Secreting histamine
Explanation: Cross-linking of IgE on mast cells triggers immediate degranulation. Histamine is released causing bronchoconstriction, vasodilation, and increased vascular permeability. This is a classic type I hypersensitivity reaction.
