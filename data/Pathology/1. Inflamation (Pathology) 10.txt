Question: Histamine is released from:
Options:
a) Plasma cells
b) Monocytes
c) Dendritic cells
d) Mast cells
Correct Option:
Ans.Mast cells
Explanation: Histamine is stored in mast cells and released early in inflammation. It causes vasodilation and increased vascular permeability. It is one of the first mediators involved in acute inflammatory response.

Options:
a) Complement C3b
b) Leukotrienes
c) Thromboxane
d) Histamine

Question: The first vascular response to injury is:
Options:
a) Slowing of the circulation
b) Venular dilatation
c) Capillary enlargement
d) Arteriolar vasoconstriction
Correct Option:
Ans. Arteriolar vasoconstrictiom
Explanation: There is an immediate transient vasoconstriction after injury. It lasts for a few seconds. This is followed by vasodilation mediated by histamine.

Question: Which of the following are thought to mediate many of the systemic  effects of inflammation, are chemotactic and stimulate adhesion molecules:

c) C3b
d) Leukotriene C4, D4 and E4
Correct Option:

Question: Leukocyte adhesion to endothelium is mediated by:
Options:
a) ICAM-1
b) P-Selectin
c) E-Selectin
d) PECAM-1
Correct Option:
Ans. ICAM-1
Explanation: ICAM-1 binds to integrins on leukocytes to cause firm adhesion. Selectins are responsible for rolling. PECAM-1 is involved in transmigration.

Question: Bradykinin is responsible for:
Options:
a) Enhancing permeability of arterioles
b) Enhancing permeability for capillaries
c) Promoting vasodilation of capillaries
d) Chemotaxis of mononuclear cells
Correct Option:
Ans. Enhancing permeability for capillaries
Explanation: Bradykinin increases vascular permeability and causes pain. It mainly acts on post-capillary venules. It is part of the kinin system in inflammation.

Question: Which of the following is not a classic sign of acute inflammation:
Options:
a) Rubor
b) Dolor
c) Collagen deposition
d) Loss of function
Correct Option:
Ans. Collagen deposition

Question: Prostaglandins produce:
Options:
a) Vasodilation, pain and fever
b) Vasoconstriction, transmigration
c) Vasodilation, vascular leakage and stasis
d) Increased vascular permeability, chemotaxis
Correct Option:
Ans. Vasodilation, pain and fever

Question: When neutrophils pass from the circulatory system to the
interstitial matrix, it is called:
Options:
a) Chemotaxis
b) Margination
c) Transmigration
d) Extravasation
Correct Option:
Ans. Transmigration
Explanation: Transmigration (diapedesis) is movement of leukocytes through endothelium. It occurs after rolling and adhesion. Mediated by PECAM-1.
