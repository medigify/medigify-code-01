Question: Concentration of alpha fetoprotein less than normal in the amniotic fluid may indicate:
Options:
a) Down syndrome
b) Liver cancer
c) CNS defect
d) Colorectal cancer
Correct Option:
Ans. Down syndrome
Explanation:  Decrease in alpha fetoprotein will indicate the possibility of down syndrome, trisomy 18, sec chromosome abnormalities and triploid.

Question: Best indicator for assessment of fetal age in first trimester:
Options:
a) CRL
b) Biparietal diameter
c) Head circumference
d) Amniotic fluid amount
Correct Option:
Ans. CRL
Explanation: Crown-rump length (CRL) is the measurement from the vertex of the skull to the midpoint between the apices of the buttocks. Because counting somites becomes difficult during the second month of development, the age of the embryo is then indicated as CRL and expressed in millimeters.

Question: Head gains half of the CRL in which week of development:
Options:
a) 9th week
b) 8th week
c) 10th week
d) 11th week
Correct Option:
Ans. 9th week
Explanation: In the 9th week or the beginning of the 3rd month, the heads constitute of half of CRL. By 5th month, it is 1/3rd and at birth the is 1/4th of CHL (crown – heel length). Hence, over time, growth of the body accelerates but the growth of the head slows down.
