Question: The diaphragm is NOT derived from which of the following:
Options:
a) Septum transversum
b) Pleuroperitoneal membranes
c) Muscular components from lateral and dorsal body walls
d) Anterior thoracic fascia
Correct Option:
Ans. Anterior thoracic fascia
Explanation: The diaphragm develops from four main components including septum transversum, pleuroperitoneal membranes, dorsal mesentery of the esophagus, and muscular ingrowth from body walls. Anterior thoracic fascia does not contribute to its formation.

Question: The pleuropericardial membranes develop into what structures of the adult:
Options:
a) Fibrous pericardium
b) Diaphragm
c) Parietal pleura
d) Visceral pleura
Correct Option:
Ans. Fibrous pericardium
Explanation: Pleuropericardial membranes separate the pericardial cavity from pleural cavities and form the fibrous pericardium in the adult.

Question: Pulmonary hypoplasia is commonly associated with which condition:
Options:
a) Hyaline membrane disease
b) Diaphragmatic hernia
c) Tracheoesophageal fistula
d) Congenital bronchial cysts
Correct Option:
Ans. Diaphragmatic hernia
Explanation: Congenital diaphragmatic hernia allows abdominal contents to enter the thoracic cavity, compressing developing lungs and resulting in pulmonary hypoplasia.

Question: A congenital diaphragmatic hernia may result from failure of:
Options:
a) Septum transversum to develop
b) Pleuroperitoneal membranes to fuse in a normal fashion
c) Pleuropericardial membrane to develop completely
d) Dorsal mesentery of the esophagus to develop
Correct Option:
Ans. Pleuroperitoneal membranes to fuse in a normal fashion
Explanation: Failure of fusion of pleuroperitoneal membranes leads to a posterolateral defect in the diaphragm, allowing herniation of abdominal contents.

Question: A Congenital diaphragmatic hernia most commonly occurs:
Options:
a) On the right anteromedial side
b) On the right posterolateral side
c) On the left anteromedial side
d) On the left posterolateral side
Correct Option:
Ans. On the left posterolateral side
Explanation: The most common type is a Bochdalek hernia, which occurs on the left posterolateral side due to delayed closure of the pleuroperitoneal membrane.

Question: A Congenital diaphragmatic hernia is usually life threatening because it is associated with:
Options:
a) Pulmonary hypoplasia
b) Physiological umbilical hernia
c) Liver hypoplasia

Correct Option:
Ans. Pulmonary hypoplasia

Options:
a) Physiological umbilical herniation
b) Esophageal hiatal hernia
c) Tetralogy of Fallot
d) Congenital diaphragmatic hernia
Correct Option:
Ans. Congenital diaphragmatic hernia
Explanation: The presentation of respiratory distress, cyanosis, scaphoid abdomen, and absent breath sounds on one side is characteristic of congenital diaphragmatic hernia.

Question: The diaphragm is innervated by the phrenic nerve, which arises from which spinal nerve roots:
Options:
a) C3, C4, C5
b) T3, T4, T5
c) T8, T9, T10
d) L1, L2, L3
Correct Option:
Ans. C3, C4, C5
Explanation: The phrenic nerve arises from C3, C4, and C5 spinal roots and provides motor and sensory innervation to the diaphragm, commonly remembered as “C3, 4, 5 keep the diaphragm alive.”
