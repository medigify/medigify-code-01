Question: Ribs are formed from which of the following:
Options:
a) Sclerotome portion of paraxial mesoderm
b) Lateral plate mesoderm
c) Ectodermal invagination
d) Endodermal migration
Correct Option:
Ans. Sclerotome portion of paraxial mesoderm
Explanation: Ribs develop from the costal processes of thoracic vertebrae, which arise from the sclerotome portion of paraxial mesoderm.

Question: Which of the following bones develop by endochondral ossification:
Options:
a) The ribs
b) The ribs and sternum
c) The ribs, sternum and clavicle
d) The ribs, sternum, clavicle and vertebrae
Correct Option:
Ans. The ribs and sternum
Explanation: Ribs and sternum develop by endochondral ossification from cartilage models. The clavicle is primarily formed by intramembranous ossification, making option B the best answer.

Question: Your 26-year-old patient has thoracic outlet syndrome, which is associated with impingement on structures passing through the superior thoracic aperture. This syndrome is associated with:
Options:
a) A depressed sternal angle
b) A depressed xiphoid process
c) Fractured 10th rib
d) A cervical (supernumerary) rib
Correct Option:
Ans. A cervical (supernumerary) rib
Explanation: Thoracic outlet syndrome is commonly caused by a cervical rib arising from the seventh cervical vertebra, which compresses neurovascular structures passing into the upper limb.

Question: Which vertebral defect is characterized by hemivertebrae:
Options:
a) Scoliosis
b) Spina bifida cystica
c) Spina bifida
d) Spina bifida occulta
Correct Option:
Ans. Scoliosis
Explanation: Hemivertebrae results from failure of one side of a vertebral body to form, leading to lateral curvature of the spine known as scoliosis.

Question: What is the name of congenital vertebral defect that involves fusion of two or more cervical vertebrae:
Options:
a) Spondylolisthesis
b) Klippel–Feil syndrome
c) Kyphosis
d) Scoliosis
Correct Option:
Ans. Klippel–Feil syndrome
Explanation: Klippel–Feil syndrome is characterized by congenital fusion of cervical vertebrae, resulting in a short neck and limited neck movement.

Question: What is the type of spina bifida characterized by a sac-like protrusion of spinal cord and meninges through an opening in the spine:
Options:
a) Occulta
b) Meningocele
c) Myelomeningocele
d) Lipomyelomeningocele
Correct Option:
Ans. Myelomeningocele
Explanation: Myelomeningocele is the most severe form of spina bifida, involving herniation of both spinal cord and meninges through a vertebral defect, forming a sac-like protrusion.
