Question: Purines / Pyrimidines are attached to sugar in a nucleotide through:
Options:
a) Amide linkage
b) N-glycosidic linkage
c) O-glycosidic linkage
d) Phosphor-di-ester linkage
e) Peptide linkage
Correct Option:
Ans. N-glycosidic linkage
Explanation: In nucleotides, the nitrogenous base attaches to the C1′ carbon of the pentose sugar via an N-glycosidic bond. In purines the bond is through N9, and in pyrimidines through N1. Lippincott chapter(chapter 22). Peptide and amide bonds occur in proteins; phosphodiester bonds connect nucleotides, not base to sugar; O-glycosidic bonds are typical of carbohydrates.

Question: Chargaff's rule states that the amount of purine must be equal to:
Options:
a) Guanine
b) Cytosine
c) Adenine
d) Thymine
e) Pyrimidine
Correct Option:
Ans. Pyrimidine
Explanation: Chargaff’s rule states A = T and G = C, so total purines (A+G) equal total pyrimidines (T+C) in double-stranded DNA. This is a fundamental concept used to explain DNA base pairing in the Lippincott DNA structure section(chapter 29).
Other options list individual bases, but the rule applies to total purines vs pyrimidines, not a single base.
