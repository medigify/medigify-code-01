Question: The largest immunoglobulin is:
Options:
a) IgA
b) IgD
c) IgE
d) IgG
e) IgM
Correct Option:
Ans. IgM
Explanation: IgM is the largest immunoglobulin because it exists as a pentamer, giving it high molecular weight and avidity. It is the first antibody produced in the primary immune response.

Question: Which apoprotein acts as an activator of lipoprotein lipase (LPL):
Options:
a) ApoC-III
b) ApoC-II
c) ApoB-100
d) ApoB-48
e) ApoE
Correct Option:
Ans. ApoC-II
Explanation: ApoC-II activates lipoprotein lipase, which is responsible for the breakdown of triglycerides in chylomicrons and VLDL. ApoC-III, in contrast, inhibits this enzyme.

Question: Colostrum contains:
Options:
a) IgA
b) IgG
c) IgM
d) IgE
e) IgD
Correct Option:
Ans. IgA
Explanation: Colostrum (breast milk in the first 2-5 days post birth) is rich in secretory IgA, which provides mucosal immunity to the newborn and protects against infections in the gastrointestinal tract.

Question: From the liver, triglycerides are transported to extrahepatic tissue by:
Options:
a) Chylomicrons
b) VLDL
c) HDL
d) LDL
e) IDL

Question: Acute phase proteins are plasma proteins secreted in response to inflammation. Which of the following does not belong to this category:
Options:
a) Albumin
b) CRP
c) Fibrinogen
d) Alpha₁-antitrypsin
Correct Option:
Ans. Albumin
Explanation: Albumin is a negative acute phase protein whose levels decrease during inflammation, whereas CRP, fibrinogen, and alpha₁-antitrypsin are positive acute phase reactants that increase.
Tags: Biochemistry

Question: Immunoglobulins attach to antigens through which structural site:
Options:
a) Light chain
b) Heavy chain
c) Fc fragment
d) Fab fragment
Correct Option:
Ans. Fab fragment
Explanation: The Fab (fragment antigen-binding) region of an immunoglobulin is responsible for binding to antigens. It contains the variable regions of both heavy and light chains, which determine antigen specificity, whereas the Fc portion mediates effector functions.
