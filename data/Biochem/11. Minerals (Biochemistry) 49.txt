Question: Copper is transported in blood firmly bound to:
Options:
a) Transferrin
b) Ceruloplasmin
c) Albumin
d) Alpha 2 macroglobulin
e) Haemoglobin
Correct Option:
Ans. Ceruloplasmin
Explanation:  Most plasma copper is transported by Ceruloplasmin, a copper-binding protein synthesized in the liver. About 90–95% of circulating copper is firmly bound to ceruloplasmin. This protein also has ferroxidase activity, helping iron metabolism. Only small fraction of copper circulates loosely bound to albumin.
