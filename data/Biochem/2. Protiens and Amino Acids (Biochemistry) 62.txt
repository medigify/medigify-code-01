a) Electrostatic bonds
b) Hydrophobic bonds
c) Hydrogen bonds
d) Disulphide bonds
e) Covalent bond
Correct Option:
Ans. Hydrogen bonds
Explanation: The α-helix secondary structure is stabilized by hydrogen bonds between the carbonyl oxygen of one peptide bond and the amide hydrogen four residues ahead. This structural feature is described in Lippincott Illustrated Reviews: Biochemistry(chapter2).

Question: All of the following globular proteins Except:
Options:
a) Albumin
b) Immunoglobulins
c) Collagen
d) Haemoglobin
e) Enzymes
Correct Option:
Ans. Collagen
Explanation: Proteins like albumin, immunoglobulins, hemoglobin, and enzymes are globular and soluble. However, collagen forms fibrous triple-helical structures, making it the exception.

Question: Which of the following combinations belongs to essential amino acids:
Options:
a) Lysine, tyrosine, arginine, and methionine
b) Leucine, tryptophan, threonine, and methionine
c) Leucine, Lysine, Alanine, and Histidine
d) Histidine, methionine, proline, and arginine
e) Histidine, methionine, phenylalanine, and tyrosine
Correct Option:
Ans.  Leucine, tryptophan, threonine, and methionine
Explanation: The essential amino acids that must be obtained from the diet are Histidine, Isoleucine, Leucine, Lysine, Methionine, Phenylalanine, Threonine, Tryptophan, and Valine. A way to remember them is through a mnemonic PVT TIM HALL.
