Question: Decrease in pH means:
Options:
a) H+ are increased
b) OH- are increased
c) H+ are decreased
d) Both H+ and OH- are increased
e) None of them
Correct Option:
Ans. H+ ions are increased
Explanation: The pH scale is a logarithmic measure of the acidity or basicity of an aqueous solution, defined as the negative base-10 logarithm of the molar concentration of hydrogen ions. Consequently, because of this inverse relationship, a decrease in pH value numerically represents a proportional increase in hydrogen ion concentration. Options suggesting that OH negative ion increases hydrogen ion decreases are incorrect because those changes would result in a higher (more alkaline) pH.

Question: The most significant buffer system, quantitatively in plasma is:
Options:
a) Phosphate buffer
b) Carbonate buffer
c) Lactic acid buffer
d) Protein buffer
e) Haemoglobin buffer
Correct Option:
Ans. Carbonate buffer
Explanation: While proteins and phosphates also act as buffers, the bicarbonate system is unique because its components are regulated by both the lungs (via carbon dioxide) and the kidneys (via bicarbonate ions). This dual-organ control makes it the primary determinant of blood pH compared to other systems.

Question: Colloidal  particles have following physiological functions except:
Options:
a) Facilitate enzyme action
b) Maintain blood pH
c) Imbibition
d) Facilitate free movement across membranes
Correct Option:
Ans. Facilitate free movement across membranes
Explanation: Colloidal particles like plasma proteins are essential for maintaining blood pH via their buffering capacity and for the process of imbibition (water absorption). However, they do not facilitate free movement across membranes; in fact, their large size prevents them from passing through semi-permeable membranes, which creates the oncotic pressure necessary to keep fluid in the blood vessels.

Question: At its isoelectric pH the protein molecule has a charge which is:
Options:
a) Negative
b) Positive
c) No net charge
d) Variable according to size of molecule
e) Same as at acidic or alkaline pH
Correct Option:
Ans. No net charge
Explanation: The isoelectric point ($pI$) is the specific pH at which a protein molecule carries an equal number of positive and negative charges, resulting in no net charge. At this pH, proteins are least soluble and do not migrate in an electric field.

Question: Sodium Bicarbonate / Carbonic Acid (NaHCO3, / H2CO3), is an important buffer system in blood. Under normal circumstances their ratio is:
Options:
a) 1:20
b) 20:1
c) 10:1
d) 1:10
e) 1:30
Correct Option:
Ans. 20:1
Explanation: According to the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation, this specific ratio is necessary to keep the blood pH at its narrow homeostatic target of approximately 7.4. A ratio of 1:20 or 1:10 would result in severe acidosis.

Question: Which one of these buffer systems is directly linked up with kidneys:
Options:
a) Na HCO3 / H2 CO3
b) Na2 HPO4 / NaHPO4
c) Na+ Pr - / H+ Pr -
d) Hb
e) All of the above
Correct Option:
Ans. Na2 HPO4 / NaHPO4
Explanation: The phosphate buffer system ($Na_2HPO_4 / NaH_2PO_4$) is the buffer system most directly linked with the kidneys because it is the primary mechanism for neutralizing and excreting hydrogen ions in the renal tubules. While the bicarbonate system is also managed by the kidneys, the phosphate system is concentrated in the urine. Haemoglobin is an intracellular buffer found in RBCs and is not directly a renal buffer system.

Question: A buffer is a combination of:
Options:
a) A strong acid and a strong base
b) A weak acid and a weak base
c) A weak acid and its salt with a strong base
d) A strong acid and its salt with a weak base
e) A weak acid and a strong acid
Correct Option:
Ans. A weak acid and its salt with a strong base
Explanation: A buffer is chemically defined as a solution that resists changes in pH and is typically composed of a weak acid and its conjugate base (salt with a strong base). This combination allows the solution to neutralize both added acids and added bases.
