Question: HIV is transmitted by all of the following ways except:
Options:
a) Unprotected sex
b) Unsafe blood transfusion
c) Unsterilized instruments
d) Saliva
Correct Option:
Ans. Saliva
Explanation: HIV is transmitted via blood, sexual contact, and contaminated instruments; saliva does not contain sufficient viral load for transmission under normal conditions.

Question: Dengue fever is transmitted by:
Options:
a) Anopheles mosquito
b) Aedes mosquito
c) Culex mosquito
d) Sandfly
Correct Option:
Ans. Aedes mosquito
Explanation: Dengue is transmitted by Aedes aegypti, a day-biting mosquito breeding in clean stagnant water.

Question: Which of the following is not a blood borne disease:
Options:
a) Hepatitis B
b) Malaria
c) HIV
d) Tetanus
Correct Option:
Ans. Tetanus
Explanation: Tetanus is transmitted through contaminated wounds (soil spores), not via blood; others can be transmitted through blood.

Question: Hepatitis C is transmitted by all of the following ways except:
Options:
a) Unprotected sex
b) Unsafe blood transfusion
c) Contaminated food
d) Parenteral route
Correct Option:
Ans. Contaminated food
Explanation: Hepatitis C spreads mainly via blood (parenteral, transfusion); it is not transmitted by fecal-oral route unlike Hepatitis A/E.
