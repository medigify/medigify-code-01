Question: The gradient of infection refers to:
Options:
a) The rate at which a pathogen spreads from person to person
b) The severity of symptoms caused by a pathogen
c) The concentration of a pathogen in the environment
d) The relationship between the pathogen and the host
Correct Option:
Ans. The rate at which a pathogen spreads from person to person
Explanation: Gradient of infection refers to the infectivity of an organism, i.e. how efficiently it spreads from one host to another within a population. It reflects the transmission potential rather than severity or environmental load.

Question: In a population of 60,000, there were 250 cases of TB on 1st January. During 1 year, 50 new cases were added. During this year, 20 died and 40 were cured. Prevalence at end of year:
Options:
a) 0.4%
b) 0.84%
c) 0.2%
d) 0.24%
Correct Option:
Ans. 0.4%
Explanation: Final cases = 250 + 50 − 20 − 40 = 240 → prevalence = 240 / 60,000 × 100 = 0.4%.
