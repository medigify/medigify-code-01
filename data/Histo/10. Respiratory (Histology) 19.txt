Question: Which of the following type of cells is a component of Blood-Air barrier:
Options:
a) Type I pneumocyte
b) Type II pneumocytes
c) Clara cells
d) Goblet cells
Correct Option:
Ans. Type I pneumocyte
Explanation: The Type I pneumocyte is an extremely thin, attenuated squamous cell that forms a major part of the blood-air barrier. This barrier, which gas must cross, consists of the pneumocyte, a fused basal lamina, and the endothelial cell of the adjacent capillary.

Question: Regulation of the size of the lumen of bronchioles is controlled by:
Options:
a) Collagen
b) Smooth muscle and elastic fibers
c) Cartilage
d) Elastic fibers and cartilage
Correct Option:
Ans. Smooth muscle and elastic fibers
Explanation: Because bronchioles lack the rigid support of cartilage, they rely on a circular layer of smooth muscle and elastic fibers to maintain their patency. The autonomic nervous system regulates this muscle to control the volume of air reaching the lungs.

Question: Which type of junctions present between type I pneumocytes and type II pneumocytes:
Options:
a) Gap junction
b) Occluding junction
c) Anchoring junction
d) Communicating junction
Correct Option:
Ans. Occluding junction
Explanation: To keep the air spaces "dry," occluding junctions seal the boundaries between pneumocytes. This prevents interstitial fluid and plasma from leaking out of the capillaries and flooding the alveoli, which would impair gas exchange.

b) Elastic fibers
c) Smooth muscle

Correct Option:
Ans. Elastic fibers
