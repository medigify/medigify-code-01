Question: A macrophage has all of the following features except:
Options:
a) An irregularly shaped nucleus
b) Many lysosomes
c) Many surface projections
d) Few microfilaments
Correct Option:
Ans. Few microfilaments
Explanation: Macrophages are highly motile "scavengers" that use phagocytosis to clear debris. To change shape and move, they require an extensive network of microfilaments (actin) and microtubules to form pseudopodia. Saying they have "few" microfilaments is histologically incorrect, as their motility depends on them.

Question: Plasma cells:
Options:
a) Contain few ribosomes
b) Have a centrally placed nucleus
c) Are specialized for production of humoral antibodies
d) Are found mostly in lymphatic tissue
Correct Option:
Ans. Are specialized for production of humoral antibodies
Explanation: Derived from B-lymphocytes, plasma cells are the primary producers of humoral antibodies (immunoglobulins). Their cytoplasm is intensely basophilic due to an abundance of Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER), which serves as the "factory" for these secretory proteins.

Question: The distinguishing feature of plasma cell is its:
Options:
a) Cartwheel nucleus
b) Site of presence
c) Shape
d) Membrane structure
Correct Option:
Ans. Cartwheel nucleus
Explanation: The cartwheel or "clock-face" appearance is the pathognomonic feature of a plasma cell. This is caused by large clumps of heterochromatin (condensed DNA) arranged at the periphery of the nucleus, alternating with lighter areas of euchromatin.

Question: Production of antibodies is the function of:
Options:
a) Fibroblasts
b) Plasma cells
c) Histiocytes
d) Mast cells
Correct Option:
Ans. Plasma cells
Explanation: While fibroblasts make fibers and mast cells release inflammatory mediators, only plasma cells are programmed to secrete antibodies. This occurs through the large-scale translation of mRNA into protein within their specialized, protein-producing RER network.

Question: Which one of the following cells located in the connective tissue arises from myeloid stem cells:
Options:
a) Pericytes
b) Eosinophils
c) Fibroblasts
d) Osteoblasts
Correct Option:
Ans. Eosinophils
Explanation: Eosinophils are white blood cells that originate from myeloid stem cells in the bone marrow. Unlike "fixed" connective tissue (CT) cells like fibroblasts which come from local mesenchyme, eosinophils migrate into the tissue from the bloodstream to respond to parasites or allergies.

Question: Loose connective tissue, in comparison with tendon, contains relatively:

c) Fewer cells and more fibers
d) More fibers and more cells
Correct Option:
Ans. Fewer cells and fewer fibers

Question: Which of the following connective tissue components is located in the ECM but not in the ground substance:
Options:
a) Collagen bundles
b) Fibronectin
c) GAGs
d) Proteoglycans
Correct Option:
Ans. Collagen bundles
Explanation: The Extracellular Matrix (ECM) is a broad term encompassing everything outside the cells: Fibers + Ground Substance. Collagen bundles are fibers; while they are part of the ECM, they are structurally distinct from the "amorphous" gel-like ground substance that surrounds them.
