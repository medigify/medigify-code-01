Question: Which of the following cell components is not surrounded by a membrane:
Options:
a) Lysosomes
b) Mitochondria
c) Nucleus
d) Nucleolus
Correct Option:
Ans. Nucleolus
Explanation: The nucleolus is a dense, non-membranous region within the nucleus. It is essentially a specialized "factory" site where ribosomal RNA (rRNA) is synthesized and combined with proteins to form ribosomal subunits before they exit to the cytoplasm.

Question: Golgi apparatus is concerned with:
Options:
a) Energy production
b) Concentration & packing of secretory products
c) Synthesis of proteins
d) Synthesis of secretory products
Correct Option:
Ans. Concentration & packing of secretory products
Explanation: The Golgi apparatus acts as the cell's finishing and packaging center. It receives proteins from the RER, chemically modifies them (glycosylation), and then concentrates and packs them into secretory vesicles for transport to their final destination.

Question: The following cell organelles are subjected to the enzyme histochemistry for the detection of acid phosphatase. Which organelle will show highest concentration of this enzyme:
Options:
a) Plasma membrane
b) Lysosomes
c) Ribosome
d) SER
Correct Option:
Ans. Lysosomes
Explanation: Lysosomes are the primary site for intracellular digestion and are characterized by an acidic internal pH. Histochemically, they are identified by the presence of acid phosphatase, an enzyme that breaks down proteins, nucleic acids, and lipids.

Question: Autophagy is the process by which cells:
Options:
a) kill themselves
b) kill their organelles
c) neutralizes toxins
d) produce inflammation
Correct Option:
Ans. kill their organelles
Explanation: Autophagy is a survival and "cleaning" mechanism where the cell sequesters its own damaged or obsolete organelles into double-membrane vesicles called autophagosomes. these are then fused with lysosomes for degradation and recycling of components.

Question: Where is the glycocalyx particularly thick in the body:
Options:
a) Brain
b) liver
c) muscles
d) intestine
Correct Option:
Ans. intestine
Explanation: The glycocalyx is a carbohydrate-rich "sugar coat" on the outer surface of the plasma membrane. It is exceptionally thick on the microvilli of the small intestine, where it houses digestive enzymes and facilitates the final stages of nutrient breakdown.

Question: Which type of cell junctions are present in the Placenta:
Options:
a) Tight junction
b) Gap junction
c) Adhering junction
d) Anchoring junction
Correct Option:
Ans. Gap junction
Explanation: Gap junctions (nexus) are communicating junctions that allow the direct passage of ions and small molecules between adjacent cells. In the placenta, they facilitate the rapid chemical signaling required for coordinated metabolic activity between fetal and maternal tissues.

Question: Which of the following organelles is basophilic:
Options:
a) RER
b) SER
c) Golgi apparatus
d) Mitochondria
Correct Option:
Ans. RER
Explanation: The Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER) is considered basophilic (stains with basic dyes like Hematoxylin). This is because it is studded with ribosomes, which contain high concentrations of acidic RNA that have a strong affinity for basic stains.

Ans. Mitochondria

Question: The transmembrane proteins in tight junctions:

Ans. Occludin & claudin

Question: Cell junction connecting basal lamina with epithelium:
Options:
a) Hemidesmosome
b) Gap junction
c) Junctional complex
d) Desmosome
Correct Option:
Ans. Hemidesmosome
Explanation: Hemidesmosomes are specialized junctions that anchor the basal surface of epithelial cells to the underlying basal lamina. Unlike desmosomes (which connect cell-to-cell), hemidesmosomes use integrins to link the cytoskeleton to the extracellular matrix.

Question: With a 5-year history of chronic respiratory infections, a 23-year-old, non-smoking man is referred to an otolaryngologist. A bronchial biopsy indicates altered structures in the epithelial cells. Which of the following, if altered to reduce function, is most likely involved in this patient's condition:
Options:
a) Hemidesmosomes
b) Cilia
c) Basolateral cell membrane folds
d) Microvilli
Correct Option:
Ans. Cilia
Explanation: In a patient with chronic respiratory infections and normal smoking history, the most likely culprit is Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia. If cilia cannot beat effectively, they fail to clear mucus and trapped pathogens, leading to recurrent bronchitis and sinusitis.

Options:
a) Anaphase
b) Cytokinesis
c) Karyokinesis
d) Metaphase
Correct Option:
Ans. Anaphase
Explanation: Anaphase is the stage of mitosis characterized by the sudden separation of sister chromatids. The spindle fibers pull the chromatids toward opposite poles of the cell, ensuring that each new daughter cell will receive an identical set of chromosomes.
