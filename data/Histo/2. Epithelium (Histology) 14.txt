Question: The oesophagus is lined by:
Options:
a) Simple columnar epithelium
b) Simple cuboidal epithelium
c) Stratified squamous keratinized epithelium
d) Stratified squamous non-keratinized epithelium
Correct Option:
Ans. Stratified squamous non-keratinized epithelium
Explanation: The oesophagus requires a "tough but moist" lining to handle the friction of food boluses. Stratified squamous non-keratinized epithelium provides multiple layers of protection against abrasion while remaining lubricated by mucus, unlike the dry, keratinized version found in your skin.

Question: Which example is false regarding the transitional epithelium:
Options:
a) Renal pelvis
b) Ureter
c) Urinary bladder
d) Collecting ducts
Correct Option:
Ans. Collecting ducts
Explanation: While the renal pelvis, ureter, and bladder are lined by transitional epithelium (urothelium) to handle urine volume changes, collecting ducts are strictly transport tubes. They are lined by simple cuboidal to columnar epithelium, which focuses on water reabsorption rather than stretching.

Question: Dome cells are a characteristic of which type of epithelium:
Options:
a) Simple columnar epithelium
b) Transitional epithelium
c) Simple squamous epithelium
d) Stratified squamous keratinized epithelium
Correct Option:
Ans. Transitional epithelium
Explanation: Transitional epithelium is the "shape-shifter" of the body. Its most superficial layer consists of large, dome-shaped or umbrella cells that flatten out when the bladder is full. In a contracted (empty) bladder, these cells bulge upward, giving them their characteristic histological "dome" appearance.
Tags: Histology, Block 1, Epithelium, Histology of epithelium

Question: One type of epithelium may change into another by a process called:
Options:
a) Regeneration
b) Amitosis
c) Metaplasia
d) Meiosis
Correct Option:
Ans. Metaplasia
Explanation: Metaplasia is a reversible process where one mature epithelial type is replaced by another to better withstand stress. In smokers, the delicate pseudostratified ciliated columnar respiratory epithelium turns into stratified squamous epithelium; it’s tougher, but losing those cilia means you lose the "mucus elevator" that keeps lungs clean.
Tags: Histology, Block 1, Epithelium, Histology of epithelium

Question: Pseudostratified ciliated columnar epithelium doesn't line:
Options:
a) Larynx
b) Trachea
c) Bronchi
d) Respiratory bronchiole
Correct Option:
Ans. Respiratory bronchiole

Question: Epithelial basement membrane contain which type of collagen fibrils:
Options:
a) Collagen type I
b) Collagen type II
c) Collagen type III
d) Collagen type IV
Correct Option:
Ans. Collagen type IV
Explanation: The basement membrane acts as the "velcro" between epithelium and connective tissue. It is histologically unique because it contains Type IV collagen, which does not form thick fibers like Type I, but instead creates a specialized, non-fibrillar meshwork that acts as a selective physical filter.

Question: Which of the following is true about the structure of cilia:
Options:
a) Cilia are composed of microtubules arranged in a 9+2 pattern
b) Cilia are composed of microfilaments arranged in a 9+2 pattern.
c) Cilia are composed of intermediate filaments arranged in a 9+2 pattern.
d) Cilia are composed of microtubules arranged in a 8+2 pattern.
Correct Option:
Ans. Cilia are composed of microtubules arranged in a 9+2 pattern
Explanation: Cilia (Kinocilia) are motile projections designed to move fluid or particles. Their core, known as the axoneme, has a highly organized 9+2 pattern: nine peripheral doublets of microtubules surrounding two central singletons. This arrangement is the "engine" that allows them to beat in a coordinated wave.

Question: Celiac disease is one of the malabsorptive disorder that leads to diarrhea in children. Keeping in view the functions of the following structures, which one is affected in Celiac disease:
Options:
a) Cilia
b) Kinocilia
c) Microvilli
d) Villi
Correct Option:
Ans. Microvilli
Explanation: Microvilli are microscopic, finger-like extensions of the cell membrane that form the brush border to maximize surface area for absorption. In Celiac disease, an autoimmune reaction leads to "villous atrophy"—the flattening of these structures—which effectively wipes out the microvilli and leaves the gut unable to absorb nutrients.
Tags: Histology, Block 1, Epithelium, Histology of epithelium
