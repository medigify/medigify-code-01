Question: The subcapsular (marginal) sinus of lymph nodes would normally contain:
Options:
a) Reticular cells
b) B cells
c) Erythrocytes
d) Plasma cells
Correct Option:
Ans. Erythrocytes
Explanation: The subcapsular (marginal) sinus is the initial receiving area for lymph entering the node via afferent vessels. While it primarily houses a meshwork of reticular cells and macrophages, erythrocytes can frequently be found here if the node is draining an area of recent hemorrhage or trauma.

Question: All of the following histological features are found in palatine tonsils except:
Options:
a) Lymphatic nodules
b) Those covered by a connective tissue layer
c) Crypts
d) They contribute to the immune defense system
Correct Option:
Ans. Those covered by a connective tissue layer
Explanation: Palatine tonsils are unique because they are only partially encapsulated. While they have a connective tissue layer on their deep, "unseen" side, their superficial surface is covered by epithelium (stratified squamous non-keratinized) which invaginates to form deep crypts, rather than a connective tissue capsule.

Question: Lymphatic nodules are found in each of the following except the:
Options:
a) Lymph nodes
b) Thymus
c) Tonsils
d) Spleen
Correct Option:
Ans. Thymus
Explanation: Lymphatic nodules (follicles) are the sites of B-cell proliferation and are found in secondary lymphoid organs like lymph nodes and the spleen. The thymus is a primary lymphoid organ focused strictly on T-cell maturation and education; therefore, it contains no B-cell follicles or nodules.

Question: The thymus is described by each of the following except:
Options:
a) The medulla contains reticular cells
b) The medulla contains Hassall's bodies
c) The medulla is more vascular than the cortex
d) No afferent lymphatics
Correct Option:
Ans. The medulla is more vascular than the cortex
Explanation: The thymic cortex is packed with a higher density of developing T-cells (thymocytes) and requires a more extensive blood supply to maintain the "blood-thymus barrier." In contrast, the medulla is less cellular and generally less vascular than the high-metabolism cortex.

Question: After their maturation in the thymus and release into the circulation, T lymphocytes migrate preferentially to which of the following sites:
Options:
a) Paracortex of lymph nodes
b) Cortical lymphoid nodules of lymph nodes
c) Hilus of lymph nodes
d) Lymphoid nodules of the tonsils
Correct Option:
Ans. Paracortex of lymph nodes
Explanation: The paracortex of the lymph node is formally known as the thymus-dependent zone. After T-lymphocytes "graduate" from the thymus, they migrate specifically to this region of the lymph node to await activation by antigens presented by dendritic cells.

Question: In which of the following sites do lymphocytes become immunocompetent:
Options:
a) Germinal center of secondary lymphoid nodules
b) White pulp of the spleen
c) Thymic cortex
d) Paracortex of lymph nodes
Correct Option:
Ans. Thymic cortex
Explanation: Lymphocytes undergo their most critical phase of "education" in the thymic cortex. Here, they are screened for their ability to recognize self-antigens; those that survive this positive selection become immunocompetent before moving toward the medulla for further testing.

Question: Reticular cells of epithelial origin are found in:
Options:
a) Spleen
b) Thymus
c) Lymph node
d) Peyer patches
Correct Option:
Ans. Thymus
Explanation: While most lymphoid organs use a framework of mesenchymal reticular cells, the thymus is unique. Its framework is composed of epithelioreticular cells, which are derived from the endoderm of the pharyngeal pouches and provide the essential microenvironment for T-cell development.

Question: Paracortex of lymph nodes contains:
Options:
a) Lymphocytes
b) Erythrocytes
c) Macrophages
d) Plasma cells
Correct Option:
Ans. Lymphocytes
Explanation: The paracortex is the intermediate zone of the lymph node, characterized by the presence of High Endothelial Venules (HEVs). It is almost exclusively populated by T-lymphocytes, distinguishing it from the outer cortex, which is the domain of B-lymphocytes and nodules.
