Question: The periosteum is firmly attached to compact bone by:
Options:
a) Processes from endosteal osteocytes
b) Fibers from Volkmann's canals
c) Outer circumferential lamellae
d) Sharpey's fibers
Correct Option:
Ans. Sharpey's fibers
Explanation: The periosteum is anchored to the bone by Sharpey’s fibers. These are thick bundles of Type I collagen that extend from the fibrous layer of the periosteum and "pin" themselves into the outer circumferential lamellae of the bone matrix.

Question: A principal difference between spongy and compact bone is the:
Options:
a) Composition of the amorphous ground substance
b) Amount of collagen in the matrix
c) Presence or absence of osseous lamellae
d) Ratio of bone matrix to soft tissue spaces
Correct Option:
Ans. Ratio of bone matrix to soft tissue spaces
Explanation: The primary difference lies in the ratio of matrix to soft tissue. Compact bone is a dense mass with only tiny vascular canals, while spongy (cancellous) bone is a light network of thin trabeculae with large, marrow-filled spaces between them.

Question: What is the space that an osteocyte rests in:
Options:
a) Canaliculi
b) Sharpey's fibers
c) Trabeculae
d) Lacuna
Correct Option:
Ans. Lacuna
Explanation: An osteocyte (a mature bone cell) resides in a small, fluid-filled space called a lacuna. Because the surrounding bone matrix is calcified and hard, the cell must sit in this "little lake" to survive and maintain the surrounding tissue.

b) Thin and flat
c) Composed of a network of trabeculae

Correct Option:
Ans. Composed of a network of trabeculae
Explanation: Cancellous bone is defined by its porous, honeycombed appearance. It consists of a branching network of bony spicules called trabeculae, which are oriented along lines of mechanical stress to provide strength without adding excessive weight.

Question: The Haversian canals of bone tissue:
Options:

b) Are not common in spongy bone trabeculae
c) Form the central structures of a Haversian system
d) Run perpendicular to the long axis of the bone
Correct Option:

Explanation: The Haversian canal is the central axis of an osteon. It runs longitudinally through the bone and contains the blood vessels, nerves, and lymphatics that provide the life-support system for the surrounding concentric lamellae.

b) Bone synthesis by the endosteum
c) Bone deposition by the periosteum

Correct Option:
Ans. Bone deposition by the periosteum

Ans. Extracellular matrix

Question: In healthy bone canaliculi are likely to contain which one of the following:
Options:
a) Capillaries
b) Osteocytic processes
c) Osteoid
d) Osteoclasts in resorption lacunae
Correct Option:
Ans. Osteocytic processes
Explanation: Canaliculi are tiny, thread-like tunnels that radiate from lacunae. They contain the cytoplasmic processes of osteocytes, which reach out to touch neighboring cells, allowing for the exchange of nutrients and chemical signals via gap junctions.
