Question: The type of vessels present in tissues where there is extensive molecular exchange with blood are:
Options:
a) Fenestrated capillaries
b) Sinusoidal capillaries
c) Continuous capillaries
d) Meta-arterioles
Correct Option:
Ans. Fenestrated capillaries
Explanation: These are the "sieves" of the circulatory system. They feature small pores (fenestrae) in the endothelial cells that allow for high-speed transport of small molecules and proteins. You'll find them where the "traffic" between blood and tissue is heaviest, like in the kidneys or endocrine glands.

Question: Fenestrated capillaries have:
Options:
a) Extremely attenuated areas of endothelium with pores
b) Thickened areas with pores
c) A discontinuous basal lamina
d) Pores that are evenly spaced over entire surface of endothelium
Correct Option:
Ans. Pores that are evenly spaced over entire surface of endothelium
Explanation: In these vessels, the fenestrations are not random; they are organized structural features of the cell membrane, often covered by a thin diaphragm, to ensure uniform filtration across the capillary wall.

Question: Which of the following layers in the wall of a medium-sized artery contains smooth muscle:
Options:
a) Internal elastic membrane
b) Tunica adventitia
c) Tunica intima
d) Tunica media
Correct Option:
Ans. Tunica media
Explanation: The tunica media is the "muscle layer" of any blood vessel. In muscular (medium) arteries, this layer is particularly thick and composed of many layers of circularly arranged smooth muscle cells to regulate blood pressure and flow.

Question: Which layer is absent in tunica intima of an artery:
Options:
a) Endothelium
b) Basement membrane
c) Internal elastic lamina
d) External elastic lamina
Correct Option:
Ans. External elastic lamina
Explanation: The tunica intima is the innermost layer. It contains the endothelium, the basement membrane, and the internal elastic lamina. The external elastic lamina is actually the border between the tunica media and the tunica adventitia.
