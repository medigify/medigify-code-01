Question: Chondrocytes are nourished by:
Options:
a) Dissolved nutrients passing through the cartilage matrix
b) A system of canaliculi between lacunae
c) The vascular network within the territorial matrix
d) Glycogen and lipid stores
Correct Option:
Ans. Dissolved nutrients passing through the cartilage matrix
Explanation: Cartilage is an avascular tissue, meaning it contains no blood vessels or nerves. Chondrocytes must survive via the diffusion of dissolved nutrients from the capillaries in the surrounding perichondrium (or synovial fluid) through the hydrated, gel-like matrix.

Question: Which type of collagen is most abundant in Hyaline cartilage:
Options:
a) Collagen type I
b) Collagen type II
c) Collagen type III
d) Collagen type IV
Correct Option:
Ans. Collagen type II
Explanation: Hyaline cartilage is characterized by a matrix dominated by Type II collagen fibrils. These fibrils are so fine and have the same refractive index as the ground substance that they appear invisible under a standard light microscope, giving the tissue its "glassy" (hyaline) look.

Question: A cartilage surface not covered by a perichondrium:
Options:
a) Is characteristic of elastic cartilage
b) Cannot exhibit interstitial growth
c) Is probably articular cartilage
d) Is probably hyaline cartilage
Correct Option:
Ans. Is probably articular cartilage
Explanation: Articular cartilage is a specialized form of hyaline cartilage that lacks a perichondrium. This absence is crucial because it provides a perfectly smooth, friction-free surface for joint movement and allows for the direct exchange of nutrients with the synovial fluid.

Question: Which type of cartilage is found in intervertebral disc:
Options:
a) Costal cartilage
b) Elastic cartilage
c) Fibrous cartilage
d) Articular cartilage
Correct Option:
Ans. Fibrous cartilage
Explanation: The intervertebral discs (specifically the annulus fibrosus) are made of fibrocartilage. This is the toughest variety of cartilage, acting as a structural hybrid that combines the shock-absorbing properties of cartilage with the tensile strength of dense connective tissue.

Question: Osteoarthritis is a chronic condition that commonly occurs during aging; involves gradual loss or changed physical properties of which of the following varieties of cartilage:
Options:
a) Costal cartilage
b) Elastic cartilage
c) Fibrous cartilage
d) Articular cartilage
Correct Option:
Ans. Articular cartilage
Explanation: Osteoarthritis involves the progressive breakdown of articular cartilage. As the matrix loses its water-binding proteoglycans, the hyaline cartilage becomes brittle and wears away, eventually leading to bone-on-bone contact within the joint.

Question: What is the primary component of fibrocartilage:
Options:
a) Collagen fibers
b) Elastic fibers
c) Reticular fibers
d) Ground substance
Correct Option:
Ans. Collagen fibers
Explanation: The defining feature of fibrocartilage is the presence of thick, visible bundles of Type I collagen fibers. These fibers are arranged in the direction of functional stress, allowing the tissue to act as a heavy-duty "buffer" in areas like the pubic symphysis.

Question: What is the term for the spaces in which chondrocytes are located in fibrocartilage:
Options:
a) Lacunae
b) Canaliculi
c) Haversian canals
d) Volkmann's canals
Correct Option:
Ans. Lacunae
Explanation: Just like in bone and other cartilage types, the chondrocytes of fibrocartilage are housed in small cavities called lacunae. In fibrocartilage, these lacunae are often smaller and more scattered compared to the large "isogenous groups" seen in hyaline cartilage.

Question: In which type of cartilage, chondrocytes are arranged in rows:
Options:
a) Hyaline cartilage
b) Elastic cartilage
c) Fibrocartilage
d) Articular cartilage
Correct Option:
Ans. Fibrocartilage
Explanation: A hallmark histological feature of fibrocartilage is that the chondrocytes are squeezed into long, parallel rows. This "string-of-pearls" appearance is created by the massive bundles of Type I collagen, which force the cells into narrow lines between the fibers.

Question: A girl presented with ear scar after piercing ear. This scar may contain:
Options:
a) Elastin protein
b) Hyaline cartilage
c) Fibrocartilage
d) Calcified bone
Correct Option:
Ans. Elastin protein
Explanation: The pinna of the ear is composed of elastic cartilage. This tissue is identical to hyaline cartilage except that its matrix is permeated by a dense network of elastin protein fibers, which allow the ear to be deformed and then instantly snap back to its original shape.
