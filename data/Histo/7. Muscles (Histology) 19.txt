Question: Cardiac and skeletal muscle are different in that:
Options:
a) Only skeletal muscle is influenced by nerves
b) Only skeletal muscle has intercalated discs
c) Only cardiac muscle has inherent rhythmicity
d) Only cardiac muscle has transverse striations
Correct Option:
Ans. Only cardiac muscle has inherent rhythmicity
Explanation: While both cardiac and skeletal muscles are striated, cardiac muscle is unique because it possesses inherent rhythmicity. Thanks to specialized pacemaker cells (like those in the SA node), the heart can beat independently of external nerve stimulation, whereas skeletal muscle requires a signal from the somatic nervous system to move.

Question: Smooth muscle is described by each of the following except:
Options:
a) It is characterized by thick, thin, and intermediate contractile filaments
b) It has a sarcomere arrangement similar to skeletal muscle
c) It can only be stimulated by nerves of the autonomic nervous system
d) Contraction is regulated by concentration of calcium ions
Correct Option:
Ans. It has a sarcomere arrangement similar to skeletal muscle
Explanation: Smooth muscle is "smooth" specifically because it lacks the highly organized sarcomere arrangement found in striated muscle. Instead of being anchored to Z-lines, the actin and myosin filaments are attached to dense bodies. Furthermore, smooth muscle contains stable thick filaments that only assemble when the muscle is ready to contract.

Question: A transverse sarcomere section through the I-band of a skeletal muscle would include:
Options:
a) Actin myofilaments only
b) Myosin myofilaments only
c) Both actin and myosin myofilaments
d) Z-lines and actin myofilaments
Correct Option:
Ans. Actin myofilaments only
Explanation: In a transverse section of the I-band, you would see actin myofilaments only. The "I" stands for isotropic, and because this region lacks the thick myosin filaments, it appears light under the microscope. The myosin filaments are confined to the darker A-band.

Question: The ability of skeletal muscle fibers to regenerate following injury is best described as:
Options:
a) Nonexistent
b) Quite limited
c) Moderately well developed
d) Extensive
Correct Option:
Ans. Quite limited
Explanation: The regenerative capacity of skeletal muscle is quite limited. It relies on a small population of satellite cells (inactive myoblasts) tucked under the external lamina. While they can repair minor damage, extensive injuries are typically replaced by non-functional fibrous scar tissue.

Question: Thin filaments have all of the following components except:
Options:
a) Myosin
b) Troponin C
c) F-actin
d) Troponin I
Correct Option:
Ans. Myosin
Explanation: The thin filament is a complex of three proteins: F-actin, tropomyosin, and troponin (subunits C, I, and T). Myosin is the sole component of the thick filament. Think of actin as the "rope" and myosin as the "hand" that pulls it; they are parts of two different teams.

Question: The contraction of the myofibril in skeletal muscle takes place:
Options:
a) At the H band
b) At the M band
c) At the I band
d) Between two Z bands
Correct Option:
Ans. Between two Z bands
Explanation: The sarcomere is the functional unit of contraction, defined as the distance between two Z-lines. During contraction, the filaments slide over each other (the sliding filament theory); as a result, the distance between Z-lines decreases and the I-band shortens, but the dark A-band remains constant in length.

Question: Which part of the skeletal muscle fiber is most closely associated with ATPase activity:
Options:
a) Actin
b) T tubule
c) Light myosin chain
d) Heavy myosin chain
Correct Option:
Ans. Heavy myosin chain
Explanation: The heavy myosin chain contains the "head" that binds to actin and possesses ATPase activity. This enzymatic function allows the muscle to hydrolyze ATP, releasing the energy necessary to cock the myosin head and perform the "power stroke" that pulls the thin filament.

Question: Which is the best pair of characteristics you can use to distinguish the three muscle cell types from each other:
Options:
a) Cell shape and nuclear shape
b) Cell shape, nuclear position, and number
c) Cell shape and presence of intercalated discs
d) Striations
Correct Option:
Ans. Striations
Explanation: The distinguishing feature used to tell the three types of muscle apart is striations. These stripes are visible under a light microscope because of the vertical alignment of sarcomeres within thousands of parallel myofibrils, creating a repeating pattern of light and dark bands across the entire cell.

Question: The neuromuscular junction is a specialized form of synaptic contact between motor neuron axon terminals and muscle. Which of the following properties distinguishes it from a central nervous system synapse:
Options:
a) The presence of junctional folds of the postsynaptic membrane
b) The presence of synaptic vesicles within the presynaptic axon
c) The presence of a postsynaptic receptor membrane
d) The presence of synaptic cleft
Correct Option:
Ans. The presence of junctional folds of the postsynaptic membrane
Explanation: The neuromuscular junction (NMJ) is distinguished from a typical CNS synapse by the presence of junctional folds (subneural folds) on the postsynaptic muscle membrane. These deep folds are packed with acetylcholine receptors, maximizing the surface area to ensure every nerve impulse successfully triggers a contraction.

Question: The sarcoplasmic reticulum:
Options:
a) Is part of the neuromuscular junction
b) Inhibits calcium production
c) Is continuous with the T-tubules
d) Releases calcium, which allows actin to interact with myosin
Correct Option:
Ans. Releases calcium, which allows actin to interact with myosin
Explanation: In muscle histology, the Sarcoplasmic Reticulum (SR) is the specialized version of the Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum (SER). Its primary job is to act as a storage vault for calcium ions. When a nerve signal arrives, the SR floods the sarcoplasm with calcium, which "unlocks" the actin filaments for myosin to bind.

Question: Which statements is false regarding myofibril:
Options:
a) 1-3 mm is diameter
b) cylindrical thread like elements
c) Arranged transversely in the sarcoplasm
d) Show alternate light and dark bands
Correct Option:
Ans. Arranged transversely in the sarcoplasm
Explanation: Myofibrils are cylindrical, thread-like elements that run longitudinally (parallel to the long axis) through the sarcoplasm, not transversely. This longitudinal arrangement is what allows the muscle fiber to shorten efficiently along its length when the internal sarcomeres contract.

Question: Endomysium surrounds:

b) Entire muscle
c) Fascicles
d) Myofilament
Correct Option:
Ans. Individual myofibrils
Explanation: Muscle is organized like a set of Russian nesting dolls. The Endomysium is a delicate layer of reticular fibers surrounding individual muscle fibers (cells). The Perimysium is a thicker layer of connective tissue that bundles these fibers into groups called fascicles.
