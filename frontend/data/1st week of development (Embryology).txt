Question: A normal somatic cell contains a total of 46 chromosomes. What is the normal complement of chromosomes found in sperm?
Options:
a) 22 autosomes plus a sex chromosome
b) 23 autosomes plus a sex chromosome
c) 22 autosomes
d) 23 autosomes
Correct Option:
Ans: 22 autosomes plus a sex chromosome
Explanation: Mature sperm are haploid gametes containing 22 autosomes plus one sex chromosome (X or Y), totalling 23 chromosomes, but the correct complement is described as 22 autosomes + a sex chromosome in Langman’s embryology.
